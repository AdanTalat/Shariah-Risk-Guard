# ShariahGuard
### Governance, Risk & Compliance (GRC) Framework for Islamic Banking

ShariahGuard is a prototype Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) platform designed to help Islamic financial institutions assess cybersecurity and regulatory compliance through measurable Key Risk Indicators (KRIs).

The project consolidates cybersecurity regulations, governance frameworks, and Islamic banking standards into a unified compliance dashboard that calculates an Overall Regulatory Risk Score (ORRS) and generates management-ready reports.

---

## Problem Statement

Islamic banks operate under two layers of governance:

- Conventional cybersecurity and regulatory requirements
- Shariah governance requirements

Compliance is often performed manually using spreadsheets and lengthy regulatory documents, making it difficult to continuously monitor organizational risk.

ShariahGuard demonstrates how regulatory obligations can be translated into measurable cybersecurity metrics.

---

## Features

- Regulatory compliance mapping
- Interactive dashboard
- Risk scoring engine
- Key Risk Indicator (KRI) monitoring
- PDF report generation
- JSON export
- Risk visualization
- Islamic banking governance support
- Conventional banking support

## Regulatory Frameworks

The project incorporates controls from:

### Pakistani

- State Bank of Pakistan (SBP)
- PECA 2016
- National Cyber Security Policy
- nCERT Guidelines
- PTA Regulations

### International

- ISO/IEC 27001:2022
- NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0
- COBIT 2019
- PCI-DSS v4
- ISO 22301

### Islamic Banking

- SBP Shariah Governance Framework
- AAOIFI
- IFSB-16

---

## Key Risk Indicators

The current prototype evaluates multiple cybersecurity and governance indicators, including:

- Multi-Factor Authentication Coverage
- Patch Management
- Incident Reporting
- Logging Coverage
- Vendor Risk
- Backup Testing
- Risk Assessments
- Data Protection
- Privileged Access
- Shariah Governance Compliance

These indicators are weighted to calculate an Overall Regulatory Risk Score (ORRS).
